Audi: Project RattleRack

As part of a year-long collaboration with Audi México, our team designed, simulated, and manufactured a mechanical assistance system to improve the ergonomics of moving heavily loaded trolleys (up to 1300 kg) in the Puebla assembly plant.

The operators exerted excessive force to overcome the static inertia of the trolleys, causing ergonomic risks, reduced efficiency, and potential long-term health issues. To help solve this, we decided to develop RattleRack, a ratchet-and-pawl based mechanism integrated into the trolley’s handle system. This design reduces the operator's initial force required to start the motion of the trolley, improves operator ergonomics and uses cost-efficient and durable materials for minimal maintenance.

To come up with this product our team and I applied principles such as mechanism design theory, dynamics and manufacture processes to be able to create, simulate and validate our proposal. My role in this project includes the creation of the 3D model using CAD tools like Solidworks, simulation of the mechanism with Adams to evaluate the performance under 1300 kg loads and the manufacture of scaled mechanical components to test and validate the product.

Ternium-Metalsa: Modular Chassis Design for Pickup Trucks

In collaboration with Ternium (advanced steel production) and Metalsa (automotive chassis design), our team developed a modular chassis concept for Class 2a pickup trucks adaptable to internal combustion (ICE), hybrid (HEV), and battery-electric (BEV) powertrains. The project addressed the challenge of producing diverse vehicle architectures within a single manufacturing line by creating a flexible, standardized front module combined with interchangeable central structures tailored to each powertrain.

The design methodology involved the creation of CAD-based layouts, modular assembly and material optimization. Some other important steps included the structural validation using Ansys Static Structural, simulating operating conditions of the vehicle like acceleration, braking and cornering.

The project delivered a tested and functional chassis platform aligned with automotive industry trends and standards towards electrification and modularity.

Piaggio: Retrofit Powertrain Design for Utility Vehicle

This project focused on the Piaggio Ape, a three-wheeled utility vehicle widely used in Mexico as a taxi. The project aimed to compare the performance between the Ape City (internal combustion) and the Ape e-City (electric), aiming to design a retrofit powertrain kit that could equal or improve the efficiency, cost-effectiveness and sustainability of the vehicle.

To achieve this, we conducted experimental dynamometer tests on both ICE and EV vehicles to evaluate their performance, including coast-down, acceleration and extreme load tests. We also determined the rolling resistance and the aerodynamic resistance coefficient to perform the tests. Other tools like MATLAB and CarSim were used to simulate suspension elements through different driving cycles. The project successfully delivered a feasible, efficient and cost-effective retrofit kit with a new electric motor and battery system with a modular structure that simplifies installation maintenance and adaptability.
